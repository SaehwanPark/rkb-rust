ESRD_SCORE	CMS-HCC Risk Score for ESRD Status	Final, prospective risk score based on the applicable risk adjustment model for the most recently available MA payment year, renormalized so that the mean national assignable FFS risk score equals 1.0. Populated based on the beneficiary's last eligible month and =. if Final_assign =1.
Risk scores for different enrollment categories are reported in separate columns: ESRD risk scores are renormalized to the ESRD population, disabled risk scores are renormalized to the disabled population, aged/dual risk scores are renormalized to the aged/dual population, and aged/non-dual risk scores are renormalized to the aged/non-dual population. Therefore ESRD, disabled, aged/dual, and aged/non-dual risk scores are not on the same scale and are not comparable. Although there are four columns, each beneficiary will only have the risk score reported for their enrollment status in their last eligible month in the most recently available MA payment year, which may not correspond to their enrollment type in the report period. The three columns for beneficiaries' non-applicable enrollment statuses will be left blank. Beneficiaries who were not included in the most recently available payment year risk score file, or who were excluded during the report period will not have a risk score. CMS-HCC risk scores for beneficiaries who are new enrollees are based on demographic factors only, even if those beneficiaries have CMS-HCC flags.	NUM	15		-	-	-	-	-	-	-	-	-	x	x

BENE_RSK_R_SCRE_01	Monthly CMS-HCC Risk Score 1	Monthly final, prospective risk score based on the applicable risk adjustment model for the MA payment year corresponding to the report period. Only populated for Final Assignment runs.
Monthly risk scores are renormalized so that the mean national assignable FFS risk score for the beneficiary's enrollment type in that month (as indicated by the corresponding monthly enrollment flag) equals 1.0. Risk scores for different enrollment categories are renormalized separately; ESRD risk scores are renormalized to the ESRD population, disabled risk scores are renormalized to the disabled population, aged/dual risk scores are renormalized to the aged/dual population, and aged/non-dual risk scores are renormalized to the aged/non-dual population. To obtain renormalized risk scores, CMS divides the beneficiary’s risk score by the mean national assignable FFS CMS-HCC risk score for the applicable enrollment type. Prior to renormalization, the MA coding intensity adjustment is removed from risk scores for all non-ESRD months by multiplying by a factor applicable for that year (provided in the Announcement of Calendar Year MA Capitation Rates and Part C and Part D Payment Policies). Beneficiaries who were not included in the CMS-HCC risk score file will not have CMS-HCC risk score data reported for any months. Beneficiaries who are in the CMS-HCC risk score file but who are missing risk scores for particular months will not have risk scores reported for those months. CMS-HCC risk scores for beneficiaries who are new enrollees are based on demographic factors only, even if those beneficiaries have CMS-HCC flags.	NUM	15		-	-	-	-	-	-	-	-	-	x	x

Performance Year (PY) 2018 was the first year where the Shared Savings Program Beneficiary Level Research Identifiable File (RIF) was provided quarterly in addition to annually.
The quarterly files can be merged to create a file that is cumulative, containing all ACO-beneficiary combinations. However, merging the quarterly files will not capture beneficiaries that were only preliminarily assigned in that year.
Beginning with the PY 2019 Quarter 2 Beneficiary-Level RIF, the files are cumulative, with each quarterly file showing ACO-beneficiary level assignment for the current quarter as well as earlier quarters in the performance year (including the preliminary prospective period).
The PY 2019 Beneficiary-Level RIFs include ACOs that participated for the: (1) 12-month performance year from January 1, 2019, through December 31, 2019 and (2) the 6-month performance year from January 1, 2019, through June 30, 2019.
Although some ACOs are participating in a 6-month performance year, all calculations are based off of 12 months of data.
3Information on beneficiaries assigned to ACOs participating in PY 2019-A, a 6-month performance year from July 1, 2019 through December 31, 2019, can be found in separate PY 2019-A Beneficiary-Level RIFs.
ACO participation can be determined by examining the current start date recorded in the ACO Participation files, which can be found at data.cms.gov. They are labeled: "Performance Year 2019 Medicare Shared Savings Program Accountable Care Organizations"
and "Performance Year 2019 Medicare Shared Savings Program Accountable Care Organizations (As of July 1, 2019)". If an ACO exists in the "As of July 1, 2019"  file and does not have a July 1, 2019 start date, then it participated in the full 12-month performance year
from January 1, 2019, through December 31, 2019. If an ACO exists in the "As of July 1, 2019"  file with a July 1, 2019 start date, and exists in the original 2019 ACO Participation file, then it participated in the two 6-month performance years of January 1, 2019,
through June 30, 2019 and July 1, 2019, through December 31, 2019. If an ACO solely exists in the "As of July 1, 2019" file with a July 1, 2019 start date then it only participated in 2019-A, a 6-month performance year from July 1,2019 through, December 31, 2019.
Beginning in performance year 2020, ACO identifier uses the ACO ID. A crosswalk file is available to match ACO_ID with the previously used ACO_NUM (encrypted ACO identifier).
The Shared Savings Program Beneficiary-Level Research Identifiable File (RIF) is produced six times, for prospective/preliminary prospective assignment, for each of the four  quarterly assignment runs, and for the final assignment run.
The  Beneficiary-Level RIFs are cumulative  through the PY, with each file including  ACO-beneficiary level assignment from the preliminary prospective assignment run through the most recent assignment run.
Due to the cumulative nature of the PY Beneficiary-Level RIFs, if an ACO receives reports in the preliminary prospective period and later becomes a terminated ACO, that ACO will remain in the file.
Files subsequent to termination will have blank data assignment run flags for terminated ACOs.
For ACOs under prospective assignment, beneficiaries are prospectively assigned at the start of the PY with exclusions applied throughout the PY. As a result, the number of assigned beneficiaries  for Prospective Assignment ACOs will be smaller for each consecutive assignment period.
