Prior Authorization of Non-Emergent 
Hyperbaric Oxygen (HBO) Therapy 

Background 

CMS implemented a prior authorization model from 2015 until 2018 for non-emergent HBO therapy in 
Illinois, Michigan, and New Jersey that tested whether prior authorization helps reduce expenditures, 
while maintaining or improving quality of care. CMS believes prior authorization helps confirm that 
services are provided in compliance with applicable Medicare coverage, coding, and payment rules 
before services are rendered and claims are paid. 

Prior authorization for non-emergent HBO therapy was voluntary; however, if the facility or beneficiary 
elected not to submit a prior authorization request, the HBO therapy claim was subject to a prepayment 
medical review. 

Final Evaluation Report 

Final Evaluation Report: 06/19/2019 

CMS released the Evaluation of the Medicare Prior Authorization Model for Non-emergent Hyperbaric 
Oxygen (HBO): Final Report (PDF). An evaluation of the model was conducted as required by Section 
1115A of the Social Security Act. The findings indicate that prior authorization decreased HBO therapy 
use and expenditures; however, there was no significant effect on total Medicare expenditures. 
Additionally, the effects of the model on quality of care and adverse outcomes were neutral.
